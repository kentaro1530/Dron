\begin{frame}{History of $\Lambda(1405)$}
  1959 Prediction as $\bar{K}N$ bound state by R. H. Dalitz and S. F. Taun.
  \vspace{2mm} \\
  1961 Candidate was reported at Lawrence Radiation Laboratory. \\
  \hspace{5mm} Using $K^- p \rightarrow \pi^\pm \pi^\mp (\pi^\pm \Sigma^\mp)$ reaction.\\
  \hspace{5mm} Low statistics. Ambiguity of decay chain.
  \vspace{2mm} \\
  1985 High stastic experiment by R. J. Hemingway.
  \vspace{2mm} \\
  1991 $\Lambda(1405)$ mass and width by R. H. Dalitz et el.
  \vspace{2mm} \\
\end{frame}
